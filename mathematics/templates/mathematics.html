Archimedes' Method of Calculating the Area of a Circle.

This project explores finding the area of a circle
using a method similar to one used by Archimedes over 2000 years ago.

Archimedes used regular polygons—both inscribed and circumscribed—
to prove that the area of a circle must be π r2.

We will use inscribed regular polygons with up to 30 sides
to approximate the area of a circle.

A more modern method—using limits—is then used to prove that
the exact area of a circle must be π r2.

Combinatorics

♣ ♦ ♥ ♠

We will use two different methods to
calculate the probability of a
pair, two pair, and three-of-a-kind
in 5-card poker.

The first method will calculate
the probability by experiment.
By considering a large number of 5-card hands
we will be able to closely approximate the
theoretical probability of the hands
under consideration.

The second method will use theoretical ideas
to calculate the probability exactly.
